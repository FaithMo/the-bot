How Umuzi identifies and selects high-value talent

Recruitment overview

  • Since 2014 Umuzi has been building successful talent pipelines for over 30 top employer partners including leading financial services companies such as Investec and TIH, global consulting firm Deloitte, leading software development house BBD, and award winning creative and media agencies FCB and Wunderman.
  • We have low dropout rates (less than 5%) and over 80% of our graduates receive high-value offers from top employers by the end of the learnership.
  • Our success is partially due to our fit for purpose recruitment process which allows us to identify and select the highest quality candidates.
  • We receive thousands of applications every year from which we select our best candidates. For the first half of 2019 alone, we have attracted over 11,000 applicants on our online platform from which we have selected only 105 of the highest-potential hopefuls.

Online assessment and screening

  • Our integrated recruitment process starts with online competency and personality tests are taken from best industry practices.
  • The competency tests look  at indicators of a candidate’s technical competencies focusing on literacy, numeracy, sequencing, problem-solving.
  • The personality tests give us insights to the candidates psychological disposition, focusing on agreeability, professionalism, emotional stability and openness to learning.

Interviews

  • Based on the online assessments, Umuzi will select a number of candidates for a round of interviews.
  • Interviews are conducted in person for Gauteng candidates, and telephonically for those outside the province.
  • In the interview we test for evidence of the following skills:
  • Conceptual: we look for empathetic problem solvers with curious minds, keen and able to learn quickly.
  • Technical: we look for skills relevant to the chosen career track e.g. for our full-stack web dev and data science tracks we do a pseudo coding exercise, with creatives we review and discuss their portfolios.
  • Professional: we review their CV and previous experience to assess whether they are already comfortable with / are able to quickly adapt to a professional environment.
  • Cultural: we look for culture fit with both Umuzi (open, conscientious, collaborative) and our employer partners.

On-the-job assessment

  • In the final stage of the recruitment process, selected learners are invited for a one week bootcamp at the Umuzi studio in Gauteng.
  • Here, the applicants are given a brief related to their career track.
  • We also introduce them to the principles of Design Thinking and challenge them to employ these principles in their approach to the brief.
  • The bootcamp phase gives the department heads one last real-time opportunity to assess the candidate's technical skills, professionalism, ability to work in a team, conceptual thinking and cultural fit.
  • At the end of the bootcamp, applicants are expected to present how they used design thinking to approach a problem and come up with a range of solutions.
  • The head of each department will make the final assessment on the candidates that are ready for the opportunity.

Employer partner final interview (optional)

  • In some cases, our employer partners will request to conduct their own round of interviews to assess our selected candidates.
  • This helps to ensure that there is alignment between Umuzi’s selection criteria and the expectations of the employer.
